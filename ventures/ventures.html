Ventures
This is the place where it all started!
The birthplace of SNK and a cradle of creativity! Justice Pratap Singh Auditorium of SSN College of engineering has been the first stage for various stalwarts. Similarly, our first venture was here in this prestigious Auditorium where we performed in front of a housefull audience.
Damaal Dumeel Damil


  Director(s): Vishnu, Parthiban

  Script Writer(s): Vishnu, Parthiban

  Synopsis: A group of people who speak various languages find
                   a bomb in a Tea shop.
                   A comedy which reflects various dialects of
                   Tamil and its subtleties.
The debut play of the 2-play show hosted in SSN Justice Pratap Singh Auditorium. The play became a runaway success and was again staged in the same venue during SSN's culturals "Instincts". This play marked the start of the success streak for SNK.
Galatta Sammandham


    Director(s): Vishnu, Parthiban

    Script Writer(s): Vishnu, Parthiban

    Synopsis: A breezy love story that probes into issues of love,
                     religion and marriage.
                     A comedy which reflects the drama in a
                     secular nuclear family.
The second play of the 2-play show hosted in SSN Justice Pratap Singh Auditorium which was a smashing success both critically and commercially, and it gained momentum in the professional circuit when it was staged in "K Balachander Awards" Theatre Festival conducted by "Maham" where it won the "K Balachander Award" for "Best Script" and "Best Actress" for the iconic role of "Paaru Maami".
Pizza Muni


  Director(s): Parthiban

  Script Writer(s): Parthiban

  Synopsis: A "poli" saamiyar finds in the midst of his sermons
                   that the world is going to end.
                   A comedy which reflects the "poli" sentiments
                   in our society.
Following the success of its first show, SNK geared for its second show the next year in the same old trusty auditorium. This time we opened the 2-play show with a laughter riot embedded with satire and sarcasm. 'Apocalypse' is the right time to call Swamiji!
Poota Case


  Director(s): Vicky, Prasanna

  Script Writer(s): Vicky

  Synopsis: A crime comedy which tracks the court proceedings of
                   a murder case.
                   A loose adaptation of the Agatha Christie classic
                   "Witness for the Prosecution" in Tamil.
The second play of the 2-play show was also a runaway hit. With the unusual combination of mystery and humour, the audience were enraptured. The brilliance of Agatha Christie on stage was magical and signalled the second straight successful outing for SNK in a row.
Seiveergala


  Director(s): Vicky

  Script Writer(s): Vicky

  Synopsis: A political satire which travels around the demise of a
                   a local market leader.
                   A satire which accurately depicts the current
                   political scene in Tamil Nadu.
A play which was a milestone for SNK as the play got critical acclaim from the likes of "Putchutney" Rajmohan. The audience were on their feet for both the shows as they cried with laughter. This "South Park" and "Cho" inspired script was not meant to laugh only.
Ippadiku Anu


  Director(s): Vicky

  Script Writer(s): Vicky

  Synopsis: Drama which revolves around the life of a village girl.
                   Essentially, a feminine take on the problems
                   faced by farmers.
Short 10 minute play staged in the professional stage "Short+Sweet" theatre festival. The play is a roller coaster ride of emotions as our protagonist Anu takes us through her life. Emphasising on the social issues faced by farmers, the emotion and sentiment hits home in taking the message to the common man.
